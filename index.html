Pollution

1. Air Pollution
Deaths Attributed: Air pollution contributes to an estimated 7 million premature deaths annually, according to the World Health Organization (WHO).
Major Sources: Emissions from industrial activities, vehicles, burning of fossil fuels, and household energy sources.
Most Affected Regions: South Asia and East Asia have the highest levels of particulate matter pollution (PM2.5), significantly exceeding safe levels.

2. Plastic Pollution
Production: Around 400 million tons of plastic are produced every year, and over 8 million tons end up in oceans annually.
Impact on Marine Life: Over 1 million seabirds and 100,000 marine mammals die each year due to plastic pollution.
Recycling Rate: Only about 9% of all plastic waste ever produced has been recycled.

3. Water Pollution
Unsafe Drinking Water: Approximately 2 billion people globally lack access to safe drinking water, leading to diseases like cholera and diarrhea.
Pollution Sources: Industrial discharge, agricultural runoff (pesticides and fertilizers), and untreated sewage.
Eutrophication: Nutrient pollution from fertilizers leads to the excessive growth of algae, which depletes oxygen in water bodies and harms aquatic ecosystems.

4. Land Pollution
Waste Generation: The world generates over 2 billion tons of solid waste annually, with 33% not managed in an environmentally safe manner.
Electronic Waste (E-Waste): About 50 million tons of e-waste are produced each year, containing hazardous substances like lead and mercury.

5. Noise Pollution
Health Impacts: Prolonged exposure to noise pollution can cause hearing loss, cardiovascular issues, and stress-related conditions.
Urban Areas: Traffic and industrial noise levels in many major cities exceed the recommended safe limit of 55 decibels during the day.

6. Greenhouse Gas Emissions
Global Emissions: The world emits roughly 50 billion tons of CO2-equivalent greenhouse gases annually.
Top Contributors: China (30%), the United States (15%), and the European Union (9%) are among the largest emitters.
