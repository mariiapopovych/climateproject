Climate Change Awareness

Understanding the impact of climate change on our planet.

Sea Level

Global Sea Level Increase
Since 1880, sea levels have risen by 20-23 cm.
By 2100, sea levels could rise by 0.6 to 1.1 meters if global warming continues.

Impact on Coastal Populations
Who’s at Risk?
600 million people live within 10 meters of the current sea level.
By 2050, 150 million people might have to move due to flooding.
Major Cities in Danger
Cities like New York and Shanghai could face severe flooding!

Erosion & Habitat Loss
20-25% of coastlines are eroding.
Mangroves and coral reefs are disappearing quickly, which are crucial for protecting our coasts!

Saltwater Intrusion
Could affect 1 billion people's drinking water.
Reduces crop yields by up to 30%, which means less food!

Economic Impact
What Could It Cost?
Small islands might lose up to 80% of their land.

Biodiversity

Genetic Variation Loss
By 2100, up to 50% of species may lose genetic diversity, reducing their survival chances and harming ecosystems.

Plant Extinction
By 2050, 20-30% of plant species risk extinction due to changing temperatures and rainfall.

Crop Yields
Rising temperatures could cut crop yields by up to 25% in some areas, endangering food security and biodiversity.

Solar

Contribution to Clean Energy Transition
By replacing fossil fuel-based energy systems with solar-powered systems, we can reduce the carbon footprint of energy generation significantly.

Advancements in Solar Technology
Photovoltaics (PV): Modern PV panels convert sunlight directly into electricity with high efficiency and decreasing costs.

Scalability and Accessibility
Off-grid solar solutions are instrumental in providing energy to remote areas, reducing dependency on diesel generators and other polluting sources.

Mitigation of Climate Change Impacts
It also reduces reliance on finite natural resources, such as coal and natural gas, which contribute to both climate change and environmental degradation.

Pollution

1. Air Pollution
- 7 million people die early each year due to polluted air, especially in South & East Asia.

2. Plastic Pollution
- 400 million tons of plastic are produced every year, with 8 million tons ending up in the ocean.
- This results in the death of 1 million seabirds and 100,000 marine animals annually.
- Only 9% of plastic is recycled.

3. Water Pollution
- 2 billion people lack access to clean drinking water, leading to diseases like cholera.
- Fertilizers cause rapid algae growth, which harms fish and plants.

4. Noise Pollution
- Many cities exceed the safe noise level of 55 decibels.

5. Greenhouse Gases
- Human activities have already warmed the planet by 1.1°C, increasing the risk of severe climate change.

Glaciers

Global Glacier Melt
- 267 gigatons of ice lost yearly over the last 20 years.
- Melting glaciers contribute to 20-30% of sea level rise.

Most Affected Regions
- Himalayas: Threatens water supply for 1.3 billion people.
- Alps: Lost over 50% of ice since 1850.
- Greenland & Antarctica: Greenland loses 280 billion tons/year; Antarctica loses 150 billion tons/year.

Causes of Glacier Melting
- Black Carbon: Soot on ice accelerates melting.
- Feedback Loops: Melting exposes dark surfaces, which absorb more heat and speed up ice loss.
