Pollution

1. Air Pollution
Deaths: Air pollution causes about 7 million early deaths each year (WHO).
Sources: Factories, cars, burning fossil fuels, and home energy.
Affected Areas: South and East Asia have the worst air pollution.

2. Plastic Pollution
Production: 400 million tons of plastic are made yearly; 8 million tons end up in oceans.
Marine Impact: Over 1 million seabirds and 100,000 marine animals die yearly from plastic.
Recycling: Only 9% of plastic waste is recycled.

3. Water Pollution
Unsafe Water: 2 billion people lack safe drinking water, causing diseases like cholera.
Sources: Factory waste, farm runoff, and untreated sewage.
Eutrophication: Fertilizer pollution causes algae growth, depleting oxygen in water.

4. Land Pollution
Waste: Over 2 billion tons of solid waste are made yearly; 33% is not safely managed.
E-Waste: 50 million tons of electronic waste are produced yearly, containing harmful substances.

5. Noise Pollution
Health: Long-term noise can cause hearing loss, heart issues, and stress.
Cities: Traffic and industrial noise in many cities exceed safe levels.

6. Greenhouse Gas Emissions
Emissions: The world emits about 50 billion tons of CO2-equivalent gases yearly.
Top Emitters: China (30%), the US (15%), and the EU (9%).
Temperature Rise: CO2 emissions have raised global temperatures by about 1.1°C since pre-industrial times.

Glaciers

1. Global Glacier Melt
Ice Loss: Glaciers lose about 267 gigatons of ice yearly, with the rate doubling since the early 2000s.
Sea Level Rise: Melting glaciers cause 20-30% of the annual sea level rise.
Impact: If all glaciers melted, sea levels would rise by about 60 cm (2 feet).

2. Most Affected Regions
Himalayas: Melting glaciers threaten water for 1.3 billion people in Asia.
Alps: European glaciers have lost over 50% of their volume since 1850.
Greenland and Antarctica: Greenland loses 280 billion tons of ice yearly, Antarctica 150 billion tons.

3. Impacts of Glacier Melt
Water Supply: Glaciers provide water during dry seasons; their loss threatens drinking water, farming, and hydropower.
Ecosystems: Melting glaciers harm freshwater ecosystems and cold-water species.
Natural Disasters: Melting can cause glacial lakes to burst, causing floods.

4. Causes of Glacier Melting
Rising Temperatures: Global warming is the main cause, with temperatures up by about 1.1°C since pre-industrial times.
Black Carbon: Soot from burning fuels settles on ice, speeding up melting.
Feedback Loops: Melting exposes darker surfaces, which absorb more heat and speed up melting.

5. Future Trends
Continued Loss: If temperatures keep rising, up to 90% of smaller glaciers could disappear by the end of the century.
Sea Level Impact: Continued melting could add 10-20 cm (4-8 inches) to sea levels by 2100, increasing flood risks.
